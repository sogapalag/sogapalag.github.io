How to hit codeforces rating 2000 in half year

Posted on 2019-10-18

From 0 to 2000

0 means competitive programming newbie. Although I graduate from CS colledge, then after waste another several years, I didn’t remember anything useful. I’ve writen less 1000 lines code, in two years before start contesting. I don’t familiar dijkstra or any basic algorithms. So I’m totally newbie on coding.

Well, I’m not 0 much, since I do have some math background.

keep practicing, keep contesting, keep upsolving. In the beginning, you need to be familiar plenty of basic algos. dfs/bfs, double pointer, divide&conquer, etc.. and ds like dsu, these might help you to hit 1600.

then you need to familiar BIT(Fenwick), segment-tree, bitmask dp.. this might help you to hit 1800.

there’re some well-known advanced(than basic, not used very much) algos. here advanced I mean they’re not used very often as BIT. e.g. z’s algo, suffix array, mcmf, 2-sat, CHT, fft, rolling hash, kmp, spaly tree, etc..

so advice for learning, based on frequency of problems’ topics.

If you leart most algos, and pay consistent practicing, then you will hit 2000 with high probability. Although most problems is not about how to impl, is about how to cut problem to pieces, or find special properties. And if you know many counting technique or combitorial knowledge, might helpful.

Weakness

Since half year is very short time compared to time devoted by expericed contesters. You might stuck at some easy part, but well-known to the community. Coding speedness is matter, and heavy impl is painful. (English reading is cost too much time for non-native speaker)

For myself, there’re some problems is hard, but I can solve it when off-contest, some even can’t when off-contest. In general, I don’t think I perform well on contest, there’re always some problems I didn’t solve it on the contest, but solve it off-contest. So perform normal can help me to hit 2000. I believe if pay much time, you will hit higher.

By the way, half year ago, for Google kick start contest, I rank 1000~2000, but in last 3 rounds, I rank ~100.

For hard problems

there’re some problems might beyond your ability now, and you can’t measure whether you can solve it or not. Well I believe more than 80% although you can’t understand in first place, but keep reading editorial, read others’ comments, read others’ code, you will get the idea. It’s all about logic, once you get the key logic, everything is easy then.
